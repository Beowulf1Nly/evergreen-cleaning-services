🐛 Critical Bugs

These are broken — users literally cannot complete the intended action.

FAQ page is entirely invisible — all content hidden by CSS
Critical
Every section below the hero banner on faq.html has the class animate-up, which sets opacity: 0; transform: translateY(28px). There is NO JavaScript on the page to add the .visible class that would make them appear. Every FAQ question, every answer, and the bottom CTA are permanently invisible to users. Someone visiting the FAQ page sees a blank green hero banner and nothing else.
faq.html
Fix: Add an IntersectionObserver (same pattern as index.html's initScrollReveals()) to faq.html, or simply remove the animate-up class from all faq.html elements entirely. The FAQ doesn't need the animation — just remove the class.
Free Lawn page cannot submit — handleSubmit() is missing
Critical
The "Claim My Free Lawn Service" submit button calls onclick="handleSubmit()" but handleSubmit() is never defined anywhere in free-lawn.html. Clicking the button throws a JavaScript ReferenceError and does absolutely nothing. No customer can claim a free mow through this page. Additionally, handleYardSize(), calPrevMonth(), and calNextMonth() are also missing — the large-yard checkbox does nothing, and the calendar cannot navigate to other months.
free-lawn.html
Fix: The JavaScript appears to have been accidentally deleted or never migrated into this file. The functions need to be written: handleSubmit() to collect form data and POST to the Apps Script, handleYardSize() to toggle between booking/quote sections, and calPrevMonth()/calNextMonth() for calendar navigation. Also add a DOMContentLoaded listener that calls initCalendar().
faq.html has a premature </body></html> mid-element
Critical
In the Service Areas section of faq.html, the document closes with </body></html> in the middle of the Citrus County card — after showing only 3 city badges (Citrus Springs, Floral City, Dunnellon). Eight more city badges (Inverness, Crystal River, Beverly Hills, Pine Ridge, Lecanto, Hernando, Homosassa, Homosassa Springs) are orphaned after the closed document and will not render. The footer is also missing from faq.html entirely — the CSS defines footer styles but there is no <footer> tag in the body.
faq.html
Fix: Find and remove the premature </body></html> tags in the middle of the file, close the div chain properly, and add a footer element matching the other pages.
Privacy policy claims "no cookies or tracking" — site uses both
Critical
The privacy policy explicitly states: "Our website does not use cookies or tracking technologies." This is false. The site sets eg_free_claimed and eg_vid cookies, uses Google Ads/Google Analytics tag, and fires tracking requests to an Apps Script analytics endpoint. This is a potential FTC, GDPR, and CCPA compliance violation — and if a customer or regulator ever checks, it's provably inaccurate.
privacy-policy.html
Fix: Update the privacy policy to accurately describe: the visitor ID cookie (eg_vid), the free-mow claim cookie (eg_free_claimed), and Google Ads remarketing. Describe what data is collected, why, and how long it's kept.
Terms of Service contradicts what customers see after booking
Critical
The Terms of Service states: "One-time services are charged at the time of booking." But both success.html pages display: "No payment needed now — we'll send an invoice after the job is done." These directly contradict each other. A customer could reasonably dispute a charge by pointing to the terms.
terms-of-service.html / success.html
Fix: Update the Terms of Service to match the actual billing flow — card is saved at booking but charged only after service is completed.
Stripe secret key is hardcoded in apps-script.txt
Critical
The live Stripe secret key (sk_live_51TLrat4J18ms...) is hardcoded directly in apps-script.txt. If this file is ever committed to version control (GitHub, etc.), shared via email, or accidentally exposed, anyone who sees it can create charges, issue refunds, and access all customer payment data through the Stripe API.
apps-script.txt
Fix: Move the Stripe secret key to Google Apps Script's PropertiesService (ScriptApp.getScriptProperties().setProperty('STRIPE_KEY', '...') and retrieve it with PropertiesService.getScriptProperties().getProperty('STRIPE_KEY')). Delete the key from apps-script.txt. Also rotate the key in the Stripe dashboard since it's been in a plaintext file.
No mobile navigation — nav links completely hidden on phones
Critical
On screens under 900px (all phones, many tablets), the nav links (Services, Reviews, FAQ) and the "Get a Quote" CTA button are hidden with display:none. There is no hamburger menu or any replacement navigation. Mobile users see only the logo and a "My Account" button. They cannot reach the FAQ, Services section, or Reviews from the navigation on mobile — which is likely the majority of your traffic.
index.html, faq.html, free-lawn.html
Fix: Add a hamburger menu (☰) button that appears at ≤900px and opens a mobile drawer or dropdown with the same links. At minimum, keep the "Get a Quote" CTA visible on mobile — it's the primary conversion button.

📈 Conversion Rate Issues

Things that are costing you bookings — visitors who leave without converting.

Hero photo is hidden on mobile
High
The hero section has two columns — a dark green left panel (your text) and a right panel with Photos/image1.png. On mobile, the right panel is display:none. Mobile visitors see nothing but a dark green background and text — no visual of actual lawn work. This removes a key trust signal for the demographic most likely booking on their phone.
Fix: Show a lawn photo above or below the hero text on mobile instead of hiding it. Even a small photo strip (120px tall) would significantly increase perceived credibility. This is probably one of the highest-ROI visual changes you can make.
No one-time service option in the quote flow
High
The hero prominently says "First Cut $19" — many visitors interpret this as a one-time offer they can try. But the quote modal step 3 only shows bi-weekly and weekly subscription plans. There is no one-time option. A potential customer who just wants to try it once and then subscribe later is forced into a recurring commitment at the first quote step, which creates friction and likely causes abandonment.
Fix: Add a one-time option at $39 (which already exists in FREQ_DATA['one-time'] — the data is there). Frame it as "Try us once, no commitment" and let the $19 promo apply. You can upsell to recurring during or after the visit.
Biggest risk-reversal is hidden inside the payment modal
High
Your strongest conversion argument — "We save your card securely with Stripe so we can bill you after your first mow. Nothing is charged until the job is done and you're happy." — only appears inside the payment step of the quote modal. Most visitors who bounce never see it. The homepage has no explicit "charged after service" or satisfaction guarantee language anywhere visible on page load.
Fix: Add "Pay After Service — Not Before" as a trust signal in the hero section, directly under the CTA button. A single line like "Your card is saved but not charged until after your first mow." would meaningfully reduce hesitation.
"Get a Quote" CTA on FAQ page may fail to open the modal
High
The "Get a Quote" button in the FAQ nav and the "Get My Free Quote" CTA at the bottom of the page use: onclick="event.preventDefault(); window.location='/index.html'; setTimeout(()=>{ if(typeof openQuoteModal==='function') openQuoteModal(); },200);". This navigates to index.html and then after 200ms tries to call openQuoteModal(). On any connection slower than instant — which is most mobile connections — index.html hasn't finished loading and executing its JavaScript in 200ms. The modal silently never opens.
Fix: Use URL params instead. Navigate to /index.html?quote=1 (which is already handled by DOMContentLoaded in index.html — it checks for ?quote=1 and auto-opens the modal). Change all FAQ CTAs to use href="/index.html?quote=1".
No hero social proof numbers (stat counters removed from HTML)
High
The code includes initStatCounters() which animates numbers like "X mows completed" or "X happy customers" — but the hero stats section (with .stat-num elements) was removed from the HTML at some point. The page currently shows only "5-Star Rated on Google" and "Next Day Available." Adding concrete social proof numbers (lawns mowed, customers served, years in business, etc.) would meaningfully boost trust.
Fix: Add back a stats bar below the hero CTA buttons. Example: "150+ Mows Completed · 5-Star Rated · Citrus County Local." The JS for animated counters already exists — just needs HTML.
Review testimonials have no photos — only letter avatars
High
All four review cards use CSS-generated circle avatars with a single letter (K, N, V, M). Real customer photos — or at minimum, a "Verified Google Review" badge — significantly increase trust. Letter avatars look like placeholder content and are easily dismissed by skeptical visitors.
Fix: Either add real customer photos (with their permission), or add a small Google logo + "Verified Review" badge to each card. The Google Reviews link at the bottom is good but the cards themselves need more credibility markers.
$19 promo not available for larger lots — no warning upfront
Medium
The hero says "First Cut from $19" but the code suppresses the $19 promo for ¾-acre and larger lots — those customers see regular full pricing in step 3. There's no warning upfront that the promo applies to smaller lots only. A customer with a larger property who clicks "Get a Quote — First Cut from $19" may feel misled when they see a different price. The word "from" in "from $19" is doing a lot of heavy lifting.
Fix: Add small print near the hero CTA: "* $19 introductory rate for standard residential lots (under ¾ acre). Larger properties quoted separately." Alternatively, change the hero to say "First Cut as Low as $19" if the "from" isn't visible enough.
Inconsistent messaging: "Same-day" vs "Next-day" availability
Medium
The Open Graph description (used when sharing on Facebook, iMessage, LinkedIn) says "Same-day availability." The meta description, hero badge, and hero trust signals all say "Next-day availability." This is a direct contradiction — someone who clicks a shared link expecting same-day service will be confused.
index.html
Fix: Update the og:description to say "Next-day availability" to match the rest of the page copy.
No review/testimonial count or aggregate shown in hero
Medium
The hero shows "5-Star Rated on Google" but doesn't say how many reviews (e.g. "5-Star Rated · 12 Google Reviews"). The more reviews you accumulate, the more this number builds trust. Right now it reads the same whether you have 1 review or 100.
Fix: Add the review count next to the star rating in the hero trust signals row. Update it when you get new reviews.
FAQ page has no Google Analytics or conversion tracking
Medium
faq.html loads the Google Ads tag but has no structured analytics beyond that. No FAQ page-level tracking events. You don't know which questions are being opened most often, whether people who visit the FAQ convert at a higher or lower rate, or how long they spend there.
Fix: Add details element toggle event listeners on faq.html to fire a trackEvt call with the question text whenever a user opens an FAQ item. This will tell you which questions matter most.
Free Lawn page is noindex — but it's a real landing page
Medium
free-lawn.html has <meta name="robots" content="noindex, nofollow"/>. This is intentional if it's only meant for paid ads traffic. But it means the page gets no organic SEO benefit, cannot be linked to from external sites in a way that Google follows, and won't appear in search results. If you want this page to rank for "free lawn service citrus county" type searches, it needs to be indexable.
Fix: If this page is only for paid/referral traffic, the noindex is correct — keep it. If you want organic traffic, change to index, follow and add canonical + OG tags.

⚖️ Legal & Compliance Issues

Copyright year is 2025 across all pages — it's 2026
High
Every page footer says "© 2025 Evergreen Lawn Care LLC." It's May 2026. While this won't cost you bookings directly, it signals to careful visitors (and Google) that the site may be outdated or poorly maintained.
index.html, faq.html, terms-of-service.html, refund-policy.html, privacy-policy.html
Fix: Change all footer copyright years to 2026. Better: use JavaScript — new Date().getFullYear() — so it updates automatically every year. The Apps Script emails already do this correctly.
Service area claims inconsistent between FAQ and footer/sections
Medium
The FAQ answer for "Do you serve my area?" says: "We serve all of Citrus County, Florida." No mention of Marion or Hernando. But the Service Areas sections on both index.html and faq.html say: "We mow lawns across Citrus, Marion & Hernando counties." A customer in Ocala (Marion County) reading the FAQ would think you don't serve them. A customer who calls and asks might get a different answer than what they read.
faq.html
Fix: Update the FAQ answer to mention all three counties: "We serve Citrus, Marion, and Hernando counties, Florida — including Crystal River, Inverness, Ocala, Dunnellon, Spring Hill, Brooksville, and surrounding areas."
/profile link missing .html extension — may 404
Medium
Multiple pages (faq.html, index.html) link to /profile without a file extension. The actual file is profile.html. Whether /profile resolves correctly depends entirely on your web server's configuration. If there's no redirect rule or directory index handling, this will 404 for users clicking "My Account."
Fix: Change all /profile links to /profile.html, or add a server redirect rule mapping /profile → /profile.html.

🔍 SEO Issues

Schema markup says 1 review — page shows 4
High
The LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema has "reviewCount": "1" and "ratingValue": "5.0". The page displays 4 reviews. Google's structured data guidelines penalize mismatches between schema markup and visible page content. This can suppress or disable rich snippets (star ratings) in search results, hurting click-through rate from Google.
index.html
Fix: Update reviewCount to 4 (or however many reviews are displayed). Also add individual Review objects under the schema for each review, including author, datePublished, and reviewBody.
No FAQ structured data (JSON-LD) on faq.html
Medium
Google supports FAQ rich results — it can show your FAQ questions and answers directly in search results, taking up more SERP real estate and driving more clicks. faq.html has no application/ld+json FAQ schema. This is a missed opportunity for a page specifically designed around questions.
faq.html
Fix: Add a FAQPage JSON-LD schema block to faq.html's <head> listing each question and answer. Google's rich result test will confirm it's working.
Hero image has no alt text (loaded as CSS background)
Medium
The hero right panel image (Photos/image1.png) is set via background-image CSS — not an <img> tag. This means: no alt text (accessibility fail, screen reader users get nothing), no native lazy loading, no LCP optimization hint to the browser, and no way for Google Images to index it.
Fix: Replace the CSS background-image with an actual <img> tag with descriptive alt text like alt="Evergreen Lawn Care professional lawn mowing in Citrus County FL". Add loading="eager" since it's above the fold, and fetchpriority="high" for LCP optimization.
og:image missing alt attribute
Low
The Open Graph image tag is missing og:image:alt. Facebook and LinkedIn use this for accessibility. Not a major issue but easy to fix.
Fix: Add <meta property="og:image:alt" content="Evergreen Lawn Care — Professional Lawn Mowing in Citrus County FL"/>

⚡ Performance & Code Optimization

Hero image not preloaded — hurts Core Web Vitals (LCP)
High
Photos/image1.png is the largest above-the-fold element (LCP candidate) but because it's loaded via CSS background-image, the browser doesn't discover it until it parses and applies CSS — long after the page starts loading. This delays the Largest Contentful Paint. Google uses LCP as a ranking signal. With image files (especially PNGs), this could be a 1–3 second LCP delay on mobile.
Fix: Add <link rel="preload" as="image" href="Photos/image1.png"> in the <head> while also migrating to an <img> tag (see SEO issue above). Also consider converting Photos/image1.png to WebP format, which can be 50–80% smaller.
data.json is fetched on every page load — for nothing
Medium
loadData() fetches data.json on every page load and calls initCarousel() and renderTestimonials(). But both #carouselTrack and #testimonialsContainer have been removed from the HTML — neither function does anything. It's a wasted network request on every page load.
index.html
Fix: Remove the loadData() call from the DOMContentLoaded handler, or comment it out. The reviews and images are hardcoded in the HTML now — no need to fetch external data.
No WebP images — PNGs are likely large
Medium
All five photos (Photos/image1.png through image5.png) are PNGs. PNG format is typically much larger than WebP for photos. A hero image could easily be 500KB–2MB as a PNG vs 80–200KB as WebP. This directly impacts page load speed, especially on mobile networks.
Fix: Convert all photos to WebP format using Squoosh, ImageOptim, or similar. Serve with a fallback: <picture><source srcset="image1.webp" type="image/webp"/><img src="image1.png"/></picture>. Target under 150KB for the hero image.
Nominatim geocoder missing required User-Agent header
Medium
The lot-size lookup uses Nominatim (OpenStreetMap's geocoding service) to convert an address to coordinates. Nominatim's usage policy requires all API requests to include a valid User-Agent header identifying the application. The fetch calls don't set this. Nominatim can rate-limit or block requests without a User-Agent, causing the lot lookup to fail silently and fall back to the static ZIP tier map — meaning customers may get wrong pricing estimates.
index.html (lookupLotSize function)
Fix: Add headers to the Nominatim fetch: headers: {'User-Agent': 'EvergreenLawnCare/1.0 (evergreenlawncareflorida.com)'}. Also consider replacing Nominatim with the Google Maps Geocoding API for more reliability (though it has cost implications).
booking_create_intent fetch has no timeout
Low
In quotePaySubmit(), the call to create a Stripe SetupIntent via the Apps Script has no AbortController timeout. If the Apps Script is slow or Google's servers are having issues (which happens occasionally), the payment step spinner will spin indefinitely with no error message shown to the user.
index.html (quotePaySubmit function)
Fix: Add an AbortController with a ~10 second timeout, matching the pattern already used for the check_customer call earlier in the same function. Show a user-friendly error message if the timeout fires.
terms-of-service.html loads Google Fonts as render-blocking
Low
Unlike all other pages which use the FOUT-safe font preload pattern (rel="preload" as="style" onload="..."), the terms of service page loads fonts with a standard blocking <link rel="stylesheet">. This means Google Fonts blocks rendering until the font file downloads — a measurable performance hit.
terms-of-service.html
Fix: Match the non-blocking font loading pattern from the other pages.
Apps Script: handleGetFreeMows() returns ALL bookings, not just free mows
Low
getFreeMowSheet() in the Apps Script simply returns getBookingsSheet() — the same main bookings sheet. So handleGetFreeMows() returns every booking row. The admin dashboard must be filtering by frequency client-side. If filtering is ever missed or broken, an admin could see all customer bookings under the "Free Mows" tab, including paying customers.
apps-script.txt
Fix: Either create a dedicated free-mow sheet, or update handleGetFreeMows() to filter returned rows server-side by Frequency === 'Free Mow'.
Apps Script: autoScheduleNext comment says "skip weekends" but code doesn't
Low
The autoScheduleNext function has a comment saying it skips weekends, but the actual logic adds a flat number of days without any weekend-skipping check. This is a misleading comment that could confuse anyone maintaining the code. If weekend-skipping is actually intended (it probably is for lawn care), the logic is broken.
apps-script.txt
Fix: Either implement weekend skipping (add a while loop that skips Saturday/Sunday after adding days), or remove the misleading comment.
Calendar shows all days as bookable — including Sundays
Low
In both calendars (main booking modal and quote modal), const isWknd = false is hardcoded. The wknd-cell disabled logic and all the weekend-related CSS styles exist but are never triggered. Customers can select Sundays as their service date. If you don't mow on Sundays, these bookings will need to be manually rescheduled.
index.html (renderCalendar, renderQCal functions)
Fix: Change const isWknd = false to const isWknd = (dayOfWeek === 0) (where 0 = Sunday in JS getDay()) to block Sundays. Adjust to also block Saturdays if needed: (dayOfWeek === 0 || dayOfWeek === 6).

✅ What's Working Well

Things done right — keep these.

4-step guided quote flow is excellent
Strong
The quote modal is well-designed: it collects address first (low friction), runs a satellite property scan animation (builds perceived value), shows personalized pricing, then collects contact info and payment last. This is best-practice quote funnel design.
Exit intent popup is properly implemented
Strong
Armed after 5 seconds, shown once per session, fires on upward mouse movement. Good offer ("Redeem $19 First Mow"). Leads directly to the quote modal. This is a solid bounce-recovery mechanism.
Google Fonts loaded non-blocking on all key pages
Strong
The preload + onload + noscript font loading pattern is correctly implemented on index.html and faq.html — no render-blocking font requests.
Stripe card collected at quote, not before — strong trust signal
Strong
The flow saves the card securely but charges after service. The "not charged today" reassurance on step 4 is a smart objection-handler. Customer autopay is opt-in. This is the right way to handle it.
Returning customer detection prevents promo abuse
Strong
The check_customer API call before payment correctly identifies repeat customers and hides the $19 promo for them. The free-lawn.html page also checks email and address for duplicates server-side. Good fraud prevention.
ArcGIS parcel lookup with multi-fallback chain is smart
Strong
The lot size detection chain (Nominatim → ArcGIS 100m → ArcGIS 250m → Apps Script → ZIP tier static map) is thoughtfully layered. Customers rarely see a "can't detect" fallback because there are 4 levels of redundancy.
Quote modal overlay-click prevention is well-done
Strong
The trackQuoteOverlayMD + handleQuoteOverlayClick pattern correctly prevents accidental modal closes when a user starts a drag inside the modal and releases outside — a subtle UX detail that's easy to get wrong.
Post-payment review request email is a great retention touch
Strong
maybeSendReviewEmail() sends a one-time review request after first payment with a direct Google review link. Sent only once per customer. This is how you grow your review count — it's already built into the system.
